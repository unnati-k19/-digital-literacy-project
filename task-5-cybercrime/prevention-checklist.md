Part B: Prevention Checklist 


Stay Safe Online: A Student’s Guide to Cyber Safety


The Golden Rule of UPI: 

Never enter your UPI PIN to receive money. A PIN is only required for making payments or checking your balance.


Verify the Source: 

If you receive a call about a refund or a prize, hang up and contact the official customer support number listed on the verified app or website.


Two-Factor Authentication (2FA): 

Enable 2FA on all social media (Instagram, WhatsApp) and email accounts to prevent unauthorized access even if your password is leaked.


Avoid Public Wi-Fi for Banking: 

Never log into your bank account or make UPI payments while connected to free public Wi-Fi (e.g., at cafes or railway stations). Use your mobile data instead.


Check the "Green Lock": 

Ensure every website where you enter credentials starts with https:// and has a padlock icon in the address bar.


Screen Sharing Apps:

Never download apps like AnyDesk or TeamViewer at the request of a "customer care agent," as these allow them to see your OTPs and PINs remotely.


Social Media Privacy:

Set your profiles to "Private" and avoid posting your phone number, birthday, or location in real-time to prevent Identity Theft.


Link Scrutiny: 

Do not click on shortened URLs (bit.ly/tinyurl) received via SMS or WhatsApp promising free internships or government subsidies without verifying them first.


🚨 How to Report Cyber Crime in India

If you or someone you know falls victim to a digital fraud:

Immediate Action: Call the National Cyber Crime Helpline at 1930.

Official Portal: File a formal complaint at cybercrime.gov.in.

Bank Notification: Immediately "Freeze" or "Block" your account/cards through your bank's official app.
