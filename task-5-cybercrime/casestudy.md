PART A- Case Study 


Title: The "Refund Request" UPI QR Code Scam

What the crime is:

UPI Payment Fraud via "Request Money" or QR code manipulation is a deceptive tactic where attackers trick victims into 

authorizing a debit from their own account under the guise of receiving a payment or a refund.


How it typically happens (Step-by-Step):

Initial Contact:

The attacker poses as a customer care executive from a popular e-commerce or food delivery app, contacting

the victim regarding a "pending refund" for a recent failed transaction.

Social Engineering: 

The scammer gains trust by quoting a realistic (though guessed) refund amount and creates a sense of urgency, stating the 

link will expire in minutes.


The Deception:

Instead of sending money, the attacker sends a "Collect Request" via a UPI app or shares a WhatsApp QR code, claiming,

"Scan this to receive your refund."


The Theft:

The victim, expecting to receive money, scans the code and enters their UPI PIN. Because a PIN is only required to send 

money, the transaction immediately debits the victim's account and transfers it to the attacker.


Who is usually targeted:

College students and young professionals who frequently use digital wallets and may be distracted or unaware that receiving 

money never requires a PIN.


Consequences:

The immediate consequence is the total loss of the transaction amount. Secondary consequences include compromised linked bank 

accounts and the psychological stress of being defrauded.
